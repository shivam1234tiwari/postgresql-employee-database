PostgreSQL Employee Database Project

This project demonstrates the creation and management of an Employee table using PostgreSQL.
It includes 50 employee records and multiple SQL queries for practice.

------------------------------------------------------------
QUERIES WITH SHORT NOTES
------------------------------------------------------------

1. SELECT * 
   → Displays all employee records.

2. SELECT specific columns
   → Shows selected fields like name, department, salary.

3. COUNT(*)
   → Counts total number of employees.

4. WHERE department = 'IT'
   → Filters employees from IT department.

5. WHERE salary > 70000
   → Shows employees earning more than 70000.

6. WHERE hire_date > '2021-01-01'
   → Employees hired after a specific date.

7. WHERE city = 'Pune'
   → Employees from a particular city.

8. BETWEEN 50000 AND 80000
   → Salary within a specific range.

9. IN ('IT','HR')
   → Filters multiple departments.

10. LIKE 'A%'
    → Names starting with letter A.

11. ORDER BY salary DESC
    → Sort employees by highest salary first.

12. ORDER BY hire_date ASC
    → Sort employees by oldest hire date.

13. MAX(salary)
    → Finds highest salary.

14. MIN(salary)
    → Finds lowest salary.

15. AVG(salary)
    → Calculates average salary.

16. SUM(salary)
    → Calculates total salary expense.

17. GROUP BY department
    → Groups employees by department.

18. AVG(salary) GROUP BY department
    → Average salary per department.

19. HAVING COUNT(*) > 5
    → Departments with more than 5 employees.

20. Subquery (MAX salary)
    → Finds employee with highest salary.

21. Subquery (MIN salary)
    → Finds employee with lowest salary.

22. salary > AVG(salary)
    → Employees earning above average.

23. UPDATE salary
    → Increases salary for IT department.

24. UPDATE city
    → Changes city from Pune to Mumbai.

25. DELETE WHERE salary < 30000
    → Deletes employees with low salary.

26. EXTRACT(YEAR FROM hire_date)
    → Filters employees hired in specific year.

27. AGE(CURRENT_DATE, hire_date)
    → Calculates years of service.

28. LIMIT 5
    → Shows top 5 records.

29. OFFSET 5
    → Skips first 5 records.

30. CASE statement
    → Categorizes salary as High, Medium, Low.

31. DISTINCT department
    → Shows unique departments.

32. DISTINCT city
    → Shows unique cities.
